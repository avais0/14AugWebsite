History of Pakistan

Pre-Partition Era (Before 1947)

Before independence, the region now known as Pakistan was part of British India. The majority of Muslims in the subcontinent wanted a separate homeland where they could live according to their own religious and cultural values. The idea was formally introduced by Allama Iqbal in 1930 during his Allahabad Address. The Pakistan Resolution was passed on 23rd March 1940 at Lahore, demanding a separate state for Muslims.

Independence (1947)

On 14th August 1947, Pakistan was created as an independent country under the leadership of Quaid-e-Azam Muhammad Ali Jinnah. Pakistan was formed from the Muslim-majority areas of British India: West Pakistan (present-day Pakistan) and East Pakistan (now Bangladesh).

Early Challenges (1947–1958)

Pakistan faced immediate challenges like refugee settlement, administrative setup, lack of resources, and the Kashmir conflict. The first constitution was adopted in 1956, declaring Pakistan the Islamic Republic of Pakistan.

Separation of East Pakistan (1971)

Political and economic differences led to the separation of East Pakistan, which became Bangladesh in 1971.

Modern Era (1971–Present)

Pakistan has seen periods of democratic and military rule. It became a nuclear power in 1998. Today, the nation continues to develop in infrastructure, technology, and education while facing challenges like poverty, corruption, and terrorism.
